Basic Grammar: Particles

Understand the building blocks of Japanese sentences.

は (wa)

The topic marker. It indicates what the sentence is about. It tells the listener, "Hey, we're talking about this thing."

Syntax

A は B です (A wa B desu)

Example

わたしはがくせいです。

Watashi wa gakusei desu.

"I am a student." (As for me, I am a student.)

が (ga)

The subject marker. It identifies the person or thing performing an action, or emphasizes the subject. It often answers the question "who?" or "what?".

Syntax

Subject が Verb (Subject ga Verb)

Example

ねこがいます。

Neko ga imasu.

"There is a cat." (A cat exists.)

を (wo / o)

The object marker. It marks the direct object of a verb, indicating the thing that the action is being done to.

Syntax

Noun を Verb (Noun o Verb)

Example

りんごをたべます。

Ringo o tabemasu.

"I eat an apple."

に (ni)

Indicates a location, destination, or specific time. It can be translated as "at," "in," "on," or "to."

Syntax

Place/Time に Verb (Place/Time ni Verb)

Example (Destination)

にほんにいきます。

Nihon ni ikimasu.

"I am going to Japan."

Example (Time)

しちじにおきます。

Shichi-ji ni okimasu.

"I wake up at 7 o'clock."

も (mo)

Means "too" or "also". It replaces the particles は (wa), が (ga), and を (o) when you want to say that something is also the case.

Syntax

Noun も (Noun mo)

Example

わたしもがくせいです。

Watashi mo gakusei desu.

"I am also a student."

の (no)

The possessive marker. It works like an apostrophe 's' in English to show ownership.

Syntax

Noun 1 の Noun 2

Example

これはわたしのほんです。

Kore wa watashi no hon desu.

"This is my book."

へ (e)

The direction marker. It is similar to に (ni) but emphasizes the direction of movement, rather than the arrival point.

Syntax

Place へ Verb of Movement

Example

えきへいきます。

Eki e ikimasu.

"I am going towards the station."

で (de)

Indicates the location of an action ("at", "in") or the means by which an action is done ("by", "with").

Syntax

Place/Means で Verb

Example (Location of Action)

としょかんでべんきょうします。

Toshokan de benkyoushimasu.

"I study at the library."

Example (Means)

バスでいきます。

Basu de ikimasu.

"I go by bus."

と (to)

Used to list nouns, meaning "and". It can also mean "with" when doing an action with someone.

Syntax

Noun 1 と Noun 2 / Person と Verb

Example (And)

パンとたまごをたべます。

Pan to tamago o tabemasu.

"I eat bread and eggs."

Example (With)

ともだちとえいがをみます。

Tomodachi to eiga o mimasu.

"I will watch a movie with a friend."

か (ka)

The question marker. Adding か to the end of a statement turns it into a question.

Syntax

Sentence か

Example

これはペンですか。

Kore wa pen desu ka.

"Is this a pen?"
